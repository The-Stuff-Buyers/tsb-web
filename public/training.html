Who We Are

The Stuff Buyers LLC is a wholesale inventory acquisition and liquidation brokerage. We connect businesses that have excess, surplus, overstock, discontinued, closeout, or unwanted inventory with buyers who will purchase that inventory in bulk.

What Does That Mean?

Think of us as the middleman between a company that has a warehouse full of product they need to move and a network of auction and resale buyers ready to buy it. We do not buy the inventory ourselves. We do not price it. We broker the deal. Our job is to make the connection as fast and painless as possible for the seller.

BidFTA (Fast Track Auctions) is our primary liquidation auction partner. They are the ones who actually evaluate, price, and auction the inventory that comes through our pipeline. Understanding who they are and how they operate is essential to your success on this team.

How We Work With BidFTA

When a seller submits inventory to us and our Managing Director approves the deal, we send a detailed quote request to BidFTA. They review the inventory details, evaluate the product, and respond with an offer that includes a price, timeline, and conditions. We review that offer and, if approved, relay it to the original seller.

⚠ Critical Rule

We never share the seller's direct contact information (phone, email) with BidFTA. BidFTA receives the seller's name and inventory details only. All communication flows through TSB. We are the bridge, and we protect our sellers' relationships.

How the Deal Pipeline Works

Every deal at TSB follows a structured pipeline. This is not optional — every piece of inventory goes through the same process, every time. The pipeline is managed by our internal system called Cyclops, which automates notifications, validations, and tracking.

The Deal Stages

01

Intake

Actor → You / Seller

Seller submits inventory info via email, web form, or in person at a trade show. Data enters the system.

G1

Gate 1 — Pending Review

Actor → Blake

Blake reviews the deal. If approved, the deal is forwarded to BidFTA for pricing.

03

Submitted to BidFTA

Actor → BidFTA

BidFTA evaluates the inventory and responds with an offer — price, timeline, and conditions.

G2

Gate 2 — Offer Review

Actor → Blake

Blake reviews BidFTA's offer. If approved, the offer is sent to the seller.

05

Offer Sent

Actor → Seller

The seller receives the offer and decides whether to accept or decline.

06

Closed

Actor → System

Deal resolved — accepted, declined, or expired. Full audit trail recorded.

The Two-Gate System

The two gates exist for quality control and seller protection. No inventory description goes to BidFTA without Blake's approval (Gate 1). No offer goes to a seller without Blake's approval (Gate 2).

Gate 1

Deal Approval

Nothing slips through accidentally. Every deal is reviewed before it goes to BidFTA.

Gate 2

Offer Approval

Every seller gets a vetted, reviewed offer. We maintain full control of our reputation.

05

Your Role: Data Entry & Deal Submission

Getting deals into the system accurately is one of the most important things you will do. Bad data means delayed deals, confused sellers, and wasted time. Good data means fast quotes and closed deals.

The 7 Required Fields

Every deal submission needs these seven pieces of information. If any are missing, the system will flag it and an auto-reply will ask the seller to fill in the gaps.

1Contact NameFull name of the person you are speaking with — the decision maker if possible.
2Company NameThe legal or commonly used name of their business.
3Email AddressPrimary email for correspondence. Double-check spelling.
4Phone NumberDirect phone number. Mobile preferred for faster contact.
5Item DescriptionWhat are they selling? Be specific: product type, brand if known, general condition.
6Est. QuantityHow many units, pallets, truckloads, etc. "A lot" is not a quantity. Get a number or a range.
7LocationWhere is the inventory physically located? City and state at minimum. Full address ideal.

Helpful Bonus Information

📸

Photos

The more the better — even phone photos are fine.

📋

Manifests / SKU Lists

Packing lists or SKU lists if available.

💰

Retail Value

Original cost or retail value if the seller knows.

📦

Condition

New in box, open box, returns, damaged, mixed.

⏱️

Timeline

How soon do they need it moved?

❓

Reason

Closing, downsizing, seasonal, overstock, etc.

Talking Points — The TSB Pitch

Whether you are at a trade show, on a phone call, or talking to someone at a networking event, these are the core messages you should be able to deliver naturally and confidently.

The 30-Second Elevator Pitch

"We're The Stuff Buyers. We help businesses liquidate their excess, overstock, closeout, and surplus inventory. If you've got a warehouse full of product you need to move, we connect you with buyers who will take it off your hands — fast. We handle the whole process for you. It's easy, simple, and fast."

Key Messages to Reinforce

▸

"We buy stuff."

This is the simplest version of what we do. The name says it all. If someone has stuff they need to get rid of, we are the people to talk to.

▸

"Zero cost to get a quote."

Submitting inventory for a quote costs nothing. There is no obligation. This removes the biggest barrier to entry.

▸

"We handle everything."

Once a seller says yes, we coordinate the entire process. They don't have to find buyers, post listings, or manage logistics.

▸

"Nationwide auction network."

Our partner BidFTA has 30+ locations. This gives sellers confidence that we have real infrastructure behind us.

▸

"Easy, simple, and fast."

Repeat this. This is the brand promise. Everything we do minimizes the seller's effort and maximizes their speed to resolution.

Common Questions & How to Answer Them

"How much will I get for my inventory?" +
"That depends on the product, quantity, and condition. But getting a quote is completely free and usually takes just a couple of business days. Let's get your info submitted and find out."
"Do you buy everything?" +
"We handle a very wide range of inventory — from consumer goods and electronics to equipment, fixtures, and industrial surplus. The best way to find out is to submit it. There's no cost and no obligation."
"How does the process work?" +
"You give us the details on what you've got. We review it, send it to our auction network for pricing, and come back to you with an offer. If you like the offer, we move forward. If not, no hard feelings."
"Who buys the stuff?" +
"Our auction partner sells to a network of thousands of buyers — resellers, small businesses, bargain retailers, you name it. They specialize in moving volume quickly."
"What does it cost me?" +
"Getting a quote costs nothing. If a deal closes, the terms are built into the offer — there are no hidden fees to you as the seller."
"How fast can you move it?" +
"Timelines vary depending on the size and type of inventory, but we typically get an initial quote back within a few business days. Once a deal is accepted, removal is coordinated quickly."

Trade Show Strategy & Field Operations

Trade shows are one of our highest-value lead generation channels. While we will always have a booth as a home base, the real work happens on the floor. Your primary mission is to walk the show, meet people, distribute materials, and get quotes submitted.

🎯 The Mission

Every person at a trade show is a potential seller. Exhibitors have inventory. Retailers have overstock. Distributors have closeouts. Manufacturers have surplus. Even attendees browsing the floor may own businesses with inventory they need to move. Your job is to talk to everyone.

Booth Operations

The booth is our home base, not our primary workspace. Always have at least one team member staffing the booth while others walk the floor.

👋

Greet & Pitch

Greet people who stop by and deliver the elevator pitch.

📄

Distribute

Hand out flyers and business cards to every visitor.

📝

Collect

Gather contact info and help visitors submit quotes via tablet or phone.

✨

Display

Keep branded materials and signage clean and visible.

Floor Operations — Walking the Show

This is where the majority of your time should be spent. The strategy is simple but requires energy and confidence.

1

Walk every aisle. Visit every booth. Do not skip anyone.

2

Introduce yourself: "Hi, I'm [Name] with The Stuff Buyers. We help businesses liquidate their excess inventory. Do you ever have overstock, closeouts, or surplus you need to move?"

3

Hand them a flyer and a business card every time, even if they say no.

4

If they express any interest, get their info immediately. Do not wait. Use the web form on your phone.

5

Ask for specifics: "What kind of product? How much? Where is it located?"

6

If they aren't the right person, ask: "Who at your company handles surplus inventory or warehouse cleanouts?"

7

Be friendly, direct, and brief. Respect their time. You are planting seeds.

Do's and Don'ts

✓ Do

  • Be friendly, direct, and professional in every interaction
  • Submit deals the same day you collect the information — speed matters
  • Double-check all contact information before submitting (especially emails)
  • Hand out flyers and business cards to everyone at trade shows — no exceptions
  • Ask for specifics: product type, quantity, condition, location
  • Use the web form whenever possible for the most reliable data entry
  • Keep your phone charged and the web form bookmarked or installed as an app
  • Follow up with Blake or the team lead if you encounter an unusual situation
  • Represent the brand with confidence — you are the face of TSB in the field

✗ Don't

  • Never promise a specific price or timeline — we do not set prices, BidFTA does
  • Never share a seller's contact info with BidFTA or any third party
  • Never skip someone at a trade show because they "don't look like they have inventory"
  • Never leave collected lead information unsubmitted at the end of the day
  • Never speak negatively about competitors — focus on our value proposition
  • Never make up answers — say "Let me get that answer for you" and follow up
  • Never discuss internal systems, AI tech, or pipeline automation with outsiders — that is proprietary
  • Never use personal email for business communication — always use @thestuffbuyers.com
